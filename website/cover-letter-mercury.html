What I love about Mercury is that it’s remaking a stodgy old institution into a perfectly tuned sports car. Banking is a clunky behemoth, a big ship that’s hard to steer. Mercury handles, it flows like quicksilver, like the name. That’s the kind of challenge I’ve spent my career solving. Across mediums, formats, and scales that the work has asked of me.

The design, the feel, and the graphics on the Mercury website are beautiful, sexy, and draw me into the information while keeping me engaged. You clearly have a talented team already, and those graphics are just begging to be animated. Has that work begun?

Since childhood, cracking open creation tools has been the constant. Painting pixels in MS-DOS Paintbrush, editing the source code of Gorillas and Nibbles to make them do things they weren’t supposed to. That instinct hasn’t changed, rather has grown with skill and experience. Always the first one in. First to learn the tool, first to break it, first to find what it’s actually good for. In this current industrial revolution, what will break through the noise, as it always has, is the well crafted product of taste.

These new tools are force multipliers, not magic wands. Taste will always prevail. Because knowing what’s right, what’s almost right, and what needs to be thrown away; that’s not a prompt. That’s over thirty-five years of refining every frame, every cut, every choice, until it’s earned. People trust my eye and my opinion because decades have gone into sharpening both. The people I work with become partners, not clients, and most of them come back. Collaborations that started as projects turned into friendships, and those friendships keep producing work.

No part of the work is off limits. At the Salt Spring Center of Yoga, the entire business, brand, and community ran through me. Operations, identity, and culture all at once, including their annual 500+ person summer retreat with a team of fifty. Social campaigns, print media, merchandise, all of it. Done with ease and a playful spirit. The work has taken me to remote corners of the world, embedded in communities to give projects a depth they wouldn’t have had otherwise.

The thing that comes most naturally is leading teams. Finding people’s greatest strengths and pushing them further. The work gets done with persistence and joy, play lives inside it, and that energy is contagious. People want to be part of it. Working with Maximum Effort proved the ability to operate at the highest level and still teach people new abilities, even freshly learned ones. When speed is required, the fastest path gets found. Problem solving is the love. The puzzle and the pressure. Directing Ben Affleck in an animation for his Eastern Congo Initiative; a film he tweeted “Here’s another film I’m proud of” on Oscar night, moments before Argo won Best Picture. 30+ years leading creative work in every discipline the job has put in front of me.

Mercury’s brand already has something rare; Taste and smooth handling. I would love to be there innovating into the future alongside the team.

US citizen based in British Columbia, experienced working remotely across time zones.
