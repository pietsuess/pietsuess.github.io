Avoid keyword stuffing.

8. Bundle Description

Write a compelling product bundle description that makes the bundle feel like the obvious choice over buying items individually. Bundle name: [BUNDLE NAME, e.g., "The Complete Skincare Starter Kit"] Items included: [ITEM 1 + brief description] [ITEM 2 + brief description] [ITEM 3 + brief description] [ITEM 4 (if applicable)] Individual total if bought separately: [PRICE] Bundle price: [PRICE] Target customer: [WHO THIS BUNDLE IS PERFECT FOR] Write: 1. **Bundle Headline** — focus on the outcome, not the savings (e.g., "Everything You Need to [DESIRED RESULT] — in One Box"). 2. **Bundle Description** (100–120 words) — explain WHY these specific items belong together. What routine, workflow, or experience do they create as a set? What result does the customer get from the complete collection that they wouldn't get from any single item? 3. **What's Inside** — list each item with a one-line description of its role in the bundle (e.g., "The Cleanser — Preps your skin by removing..."). 4. **Savings Callout** — one clear line showing the value (e.g., "Save $XX when you buy the set — that's like getting [ITEM] free"). 5. **Who It's For** — 3 bullet points describing the ideal buyer in "you" language ("You just moved into a new apartment and want to..." ). Make the bundle feel curated, not just discounted.
Tip: Bundles convert best when they solve a complete problem. Frame yours as "everything you need for [specific outcome]" rather than "3 products at a discount." The story matters more than the savings.

9. Abandoned Cart Sequence (3 Emails)

Write a 3-email abandoned cart sequence that recovers lost sales without feeling pushy or robotic. Store: [STORE NAME] Product type left in cart: [PRODUCT CATEGORY OR SPECIFIC PRODUCT] Average order value: [AMOUNT] Brand personality: [DESCRIBE IN 3-5 WORDS, e.g., "warm, playful, premium"] **Email 1 — Sent 1 hour after abandonment: "The Gentle Reminder"** - Subject line: 3 options (no spam trigger words, no ALL CAPS) - Preview text (35–45 chars) - Tone: helpful, not salesy. "Hey, you left something behind" energy - Content: acknowledge they were browsing, show the product, single clear CTA to return to cart - Do NOT offer a discount yet **Email 2 — Sent 24 hours after abandonment: "The Objection Handler"** - Subject line: 3 options - Preview text - Content: address the 2–3 most common reasons people don't complete a purchase for [PRODUCT TYPE] (shipping cost? not sure about quality? comparing options?). Include a testimonial or review quote. Add trust signals (guarantee, return policy, customer count). CTA back to cart. **Email 3 — Sent 48 hours after abandonment: "The Last Chance"** - Subject line: 3 options (create urgency) - Preview text - Content: this is their final reminder. Introduce a small incentive: [10% OFF / FREE SHIPPING / FREE GIFT]. Add a deadline to the offer (48 hours). Show the product one more time with a strong CTA. Mention that their cart will expire soon. For all 3 emails: keep body copy under 120 words. Write subject lines that feel personal, not promotional. Include a plain-text version note.
Tip: Email 1 recovers the most carts (roughly 50% of all recoveries). Don't overthink it — a clean reminder with a button often outperforms clever copy. Save the persuasion for Emails 2 and 3.

11. Post-Purchase Email

Write a post-purchase confirmation email for [PRODUCT NAME] that makes the customer feel great about their purchase and sets up the next sale. Store: [STORE NAME] Typical shipping time: [X-Y BUSINESS DAYS] Brand hashtag: [#YOURBRANDHASHTAG] Complementary product: [PRODUCT THAT PAIRS WELL WITH THEIR PURCHASE] Include these sections: 1. **Warm Confirmation** — not "Your order has been received" but something that makes them feel excited. Tip: The post-purchase window is when customer trust is highest. A great confirmation email has the highest open rate of any email you'll send (~60-70%). Use it wisely.

13. Product Review Request

Write a review request email sent [7 / 14 / 30] days after delivery of [PRODUCT NAME]. Store: [STORE NAME] Review platform: [SHOPIFY / GOOGLE / AMAZON / TRUSTPILOT / YOUR WEBSITE] Incentive: [10% DISCOUNT / LOYALTY POINTS / NONE] The email should: 1. **Open with their experience, not your ask.** Start by checking in: "How are you enjoying your [PRODUCT]?" or "You've had your [PRODUCT] for [X] days now — how's it going?" Make it about them. 2. **Explain why reviews matter (for other customers, not for you).** Frame it as helping fellow shoppers: "Your honest experience helps other [CUSTOMERS] make the right choice." Never say "help us grow" or "support our small business." 3. **Make it dead simple.** One button. One link. Direct to the review form. Takes about 30 seconds." 4. **Offer the incentive (if applicable).** "As a thank you, here's [INCENTIVE] on your next order." Keep it after the ask, not before — it's a thank you, not a bribe. 5. **Include guidance prompts.** Suggest what to mention: "What made you choose [PRODUCT]? How does it compare to what you used before? Would you recommend it to a friend?" These help customers write better reviews. Subject line: 3 options (none should contain the word "review" — spam filters flag it). Keep the email under 100 words.
Tip: The best timing depends on your product. Skincare: 14–30 days (they need results). Clothing: 7 days. Electronics: 14 days. Ask too early and they haven't formed an opinion yet.

17. Facebook / Instagram Ad Copy

Write 5 variations of Facebook/Instagram ad copy for [PRODUCT NAME + BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. Target audience: [AGE, GENDER, INTERESTS, PAIN POINTS] Price: [AMOUNT] Key benefit: [PRIMARY SELLING POINT] Landing page: [PRODUCT PAGE / COLLECTION / QUIZ / LANDING PAGE] Offer (if any): [DISCOUNT / FREE SHIPPING / NONE] Write each variation using a different hook strategy: **Variation 1 — Pain Point Hook:** Lead with the frustration your audience feels. Agitate it briefly. Present the product as the solution. Tip: Test all 5 hooks with the same creative and $5/day each for 3–5 days. Kill the bottom 3, scale the top 2. The hook that wins is often not the one you'd expect.

26. About Page Story

Write an About page for my e-commerce store that tells a genuine story and builds trust. Store: [STORE NAME] Founded: [YEAR] Origin story: [HOW AND WHY YOU STARTED — be specific: what moment, frustration, or inspiration sparked it?] What makes us different: [YOUR USP] Values: [LIST 3-5 CORE VALUES] Team: [JUST YOU / SMALL TEAM / GROWING COMPANY — brief description] Tone: [AUTHENTIC / PREMIUM / PLAYFUL / WARM] Customer count or milestone: [ANY IMPRESSIVE NUMBER] Write the About page with this structure: 1. **Opening Hook** (2–3 sentences): Don't start with "Welcome to [Brand]" or "[Brand] was founded in [YEAR]." Start with the moment, the problem, or the belief that sparked everything. Make the reader feel something in the first line. 2. **Origin Story** (100–150 words): Tell the story of how the brand came to be. Include a specific moment or turning point. Use "I" or "we" — this is personal. What did you see that others missed? What frustrated you enough to start something? 3. **What We Believe** (50–80 words): Your values and philosophy, written as convictions, not corporate statements. 27. Shipping & Returns Page

Write a clear, customer-friendly shipping and returns policy for my e-commerce store that reduces support tickets and builds purchase confidence. Store: [STORE NAME] Ships from: [CITY / COUNTRY] Shipping options: - Standard: [PRICE, DELIVERY TIME] - Express: [PRICE, DELIVERY TIME] - Free shipping threshold: [ORDER AMOUNT, IF APPLICABLE] International shipping: [YES/NO, WHICH COUNTRIES, COST] Returns: - Return window: [30 / 60 / 90 DAYS] - Condition requirements: [UNWORN / UNUSED / ORIGINAL PACKAGING / ETC.] - Who pays return shipping: [CUSTOMER / FREE RETURNS / LABEL PROVIDED] - Refund method: [ORIGINAL PAYMENT / STORE CREDIT / CHOICE] - Exchange policy: [DESCRIBE] - Non-returnable items: [LIST ANY EXCEPTIONS] Write the page in FAQ format with these sections: **Shipping:** Q: How long does shipping take? Q: How much does shipping cost? Q: Do you ship internationally? Q: How do I track my order? Q: What if my package is lost or damaged? **Returns & Exchanges:** Q: What is your return policy? Q: How do I start a return? Q: When will I get my refund? Q: Can I exchange instead of return? Q: Are there items that can't be returned? **Tone:** reassuring and straightforward. Not legalistic — no "pursuant to" or "the customer shall." Write it like you're explaining the policy to a friend. Lead every answer with the good news. Tip: A generous, clearly stated return policy increases conversions by 10–30%. Customers who know they CAN return are more likely to buy and less likely to actually return. Clarity reduces anxiety; anxiety kills conversions.

28. Exit-Intent Popup Copy

Write exit-intent popup copy for [STORE NAME] that captures leaving visitors without being annoying. Store type: [WHAT YOU SELL] Offer: [10% OFF / 15% OFF / FREE SHIPPING / FREE GIFT WITH FIRST ORDER] Discount code: [CODE] Target action: [EMAIL SIGNUP / COMPLETE PURCHASE / BOTH] **5 Headline Variations** (NOT "Wait! Don't go!"): Write headlines that give a reason to stay, not guilt for leaving. Each should use a different angle: 1. Value-focused: lead with what they'll get 2. Curiosity-focused: make them wonder 3. Social proof: what others are doing 4. FOMO: what they're about to miss 5. Benefit + offer: combine the product benefit with the incentive **Subtext for each headline** (1–2 sentences): Address the likely reason they're leaving (price, not sure, just browsing, comparing options) and counter it with the offer or a trust signal. **CTA Button Text** (5 options): Not "Submit" or "Subscribe." Tip: Exit-intent popups convert 2–5% of abandoning visitors. That's significant. But test timing: show it only after 30+ seconds on site and only to new visitors. Repeat visitors should get a different message (or none).
