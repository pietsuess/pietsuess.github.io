AI Business Starter Kit

30 ChatGPT Power Prompts for Small Businesses — Copy, paste, customize the [BRACKETED] parts.

Marketing Copy (1-8)

PROMPT 01
Landing Page Hero Copy
You are a world-class direct response copywriter. Write landing page hero copy for [YOUR BUSINESS/PRODUCT]. Target audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER] Main benefit: [THE #1 THING YOUR PRODUCT DOES FOR THEM] Tone: [professional / playful / urgent / luxurious] Write: 1. A headline (under 10 words) that leads with the benefit, not the feature 2. A subheadline (1 sentence) that addresses their biggest objection 3. 3 bullet points highlighting transformation, not features 4. A CTA button text (4 words max) Use the PAS framework (Problem-Agitate-Solution). No fluff. Every word earns its place.
This alone replaces a $500 copywriter for your landing page.
PROMPT 02
Email Welcome Sequence (5 emails)
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to [YOUR BUSINESS]. Business: [WHAT YOU SELL] Audience: [WHO THEY ARE] Goal: Convert subscriber to first purchase within 7 days. Structure each email with: - Subject line (under 45 characters, curiosity-driven) - Preview text (under 60 characters) - Body (150-200 words max) - CTA Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + brand story + quick win Email 2 (Day 1): Address their #1 pain point Email 3 (Day 3): Social proof / testimonial story Email 4 (Day 5): Overcome main objection Email 5 (Day 7): Time-limited offer + urgency Tone: conversational, like a smart friend giving advice. No corporate speak.
PROMPT 03
Social Media Content Calendar (30 days)
Create a 30-day social media content calendar for [YOUR BUSINESS] on [Instagram / TikTok / LinkedIn]. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Audience: [WHO YOU'RE TARGETING] Goal: [grow followers / drive sales / build authority] For each day provide: - Content type (Reel, Carousel, Story, Static post) - Topic/hook (the first line that stops the scroll) - Brief description (2 sentences) - Hashtag set (5-7 relevant hashtags) Follow the 4-1-1 rule: 4 value posts, 1 soft sell, 1 hard sell per cycle. Include 4 "trending audio" Reel ideas. Mark best posting times for [YOUR TIMEZONE]. Format as a clean table.
PROMPT 04
Google/Facebook Ad Copy (10 variations)
Write 10 ad copy variations for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] on [Google Ads / Facebook Ads / Instagram Ads]. Product: [WHAT IT IS] Price: [PRICE POINT] Target: [WHO IS THE BUYER] USP: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT] For each variation provide: - Headline (30 characters for Google, 40 for Facebook) - Description/primary text (90 chars Google, 125 chars Facebook) - CTA Create 3 versions using each angle: - Pain point (what problem does it solve?) - Social proof (numbers, testimonials) - Urgency (limited time/quantity) - Plus 1 wild card contrarian angle Mark which variation you'd A/B test first and why.
PROMPT 05
Product Description (E-commerce)
Write a product description for [YOUR PRODUCT] for my [Shopify / Amazon / Etsy] store. Product: [NAME AND WHAT IT IS] Key features: [LIST 3-5 FEATURES] Materials/specs: [RELEVANT DETAILS] Price: [PRICE] Target buyer: [WHO BUYS THIS] Write: 1. SEO-optimized title (under 80 characters, front-load keywords) 2. Hook paragraph (2 sentences, benefit-first) 3. 5 bullet points (feature → benefit format: "Made with X, so you get Y") 4. Closing urgency line 5. 5 backend search keywords (comma-separated) Tone: [premium / casual / technical / playful] Avoid: superlatives, ALL CAPS, exclamation marks.
PROMPT 06
Brand Voice Guide
Create a brand voice guide for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY] Values: [3 CORE VALUES] Audience: [WHO YOU TALK TO] Competitors: [2-3 COMPETITORS] Define: 1. Voice attributes (3 adjectives that describe how we sound) 2. "We say / We don't say" table (10 examples) 3. Tone spectrum: how voice shifts across contexts (social media vs. customer support vs. sales page) 4. Sample paragraph in our voice about [TOPIC] 5. Sample paragraph showing what our voice is NOT 6. One-sentence brand voice summary a new hire could memorize This should fit on one page when printed.
PROMPT 07
Customer Testimonial Request Email
Write 3 versions of an email asking customers for a testimonial/review for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Version 1: Casual and friendly (for repeat customers) Version 2: Professional (for B2B clients) Version 3: Incentivized (offering [DISCOUNT/FREEBIE] for review) Each email should: - Be under 100 words - Include a specific question that generates useful testimonials (not just "it was great") - Ask them to mention: their problem before, the solution, and the result - Include direct link placeholder [REVIEW LINK] - Have a subject line that gets opened People don't leave reviews because the ask is vague. Make it effortless.
PROMPT 08
Press Release Template
Write a press release for [YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT] for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Announcement: [WHAT'S NEW - product launch, funding, partnership, milestone] Company: [NAME, LOCATION, WHAT YOU DO] Quote from: [YOUR NAME, TITLE] Follow AP style. Structure: 1. Headline (factual, not promotional, under 70 characters) 2. Dateline (city, date) 3. Lead paragraph: who, what, when, where, why in 2 sentences 4. Supporting paragraph with context and data 5. Quote from founder/CEO (make it sound human, not corporate) 6. Additional details / availability / pricing 7. Boilerplate "About" paragraph (50 words) 8. Media contact info placeholder Total: 400 words max. Journalists skim — front-load the newsworthy part.

Strategy & Planning (9-16)

PROMPT 09
Customer Persona Builder
Build a detailed customer persona for [YOUR BUSINESS]. What I sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE] Current customers tend to be: [ANY INFO YOU HAVE] Price point: [PRICE] Create a persona with: - Name, age, occupation, income range, location - Daily routine (morning to night, where does your product fit in?) - 3 goals related to your product category - 3 frustrations / pain points - Where they spend time online (specific platforms and communities) - What they Google before buying your product (5 search queries) - Their buying trigger (what pushes them from "interested" to "purchased") - Their main objection (why they almost didn't buy) - One quote that summarizes their mindset Make this feel like a real person, not a marketing abstraction.
PROMPT 10
Competitor Analysis
Analyze my competitive landscape for [YOUR BUSINESS]. My business: [WHAT YOU DO, PRICE, TARGET MARKET] Competitors: [LIST 3-5 COMPETITORS] For each competitor, analyze: 1. Their positioning (what promise do they lead with?) 2. Their pricing strategy 3. Their strongest marketing channel 4. Their biggest weakness / gap 5. What customers complain about in their reviews (check Google, Trustpilot, Reddit) Then tell me: - Where is the white space nobody is serving? - What positioning angle would differentiate me from ALL of them? - What is one thing I should steal from each competitor? - What is one thing I should deliberately do the opposite of? Format as a comparison table + strategic recommendations.
PROMPT 11
90-Day Business Sprint Plan
Create a 90-day action plan for [YOUR BUSINESS] to achieve [YOUR GOAL]. Current state: [WHERE YOU ARE NOW - revenue, customers, stage] Goal: [SPECIFIC TARGET - e.g., "first 100 customers" or "$10k/month revenue"] Budget: [MONTHLY BUDGET FOR MARKETING/TOOLS] Time available: [HOURS PER WEEK] Break into 3 phases: - Days 1-30: Foundation (what to build/fix first) - Days 31-60: Traction (what to launch and test) - Days 61-90: Scale (what to double down on) For each phase, give: - 3 specific actions (not vague advice) - 1 key metric to track - 1 tool recommendation - 1 potential pitfall to avoid End with: "If you only do ONE thing from this plan, do ___."
PROMPT 12
Pricing Strategy Advisor
Help me set pricing for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. What I sell: [DESCRIBE IT] My costs: [COST TO PRODUCE/DELIVER] Current price: [IF YOU HAVE ONE] Competitor prices: [RANGE] Target market: [WHO BUYS IT] Analyze and recommend: 1. Cost-plus pricing (what's the minimum?) 2. Value-based pricing (what's it worth to the customer?) 3. Competitive pricing (where do I sit vs. competitors?) 4. Psychological pricing tactics (charm pricing, anchoring, bundling) 5. Tiered pricing structure (Good/Better/Best) For the tiered structure, define what each tier includes and the price for each. Tell me: what's the price most businesses would set, and what's the price a smart business would set? Explain the difference.
PROMPT 13
SWOT Analysis
Conduct a SWOT analysis for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY] Stage: [startup / growing / established] Team size: [NUMBER] Unique advantages: [WHAT YOU DO WELL] Known challenges: [WHAT YOU STRUGGLE WITH] For each quadrant, provide 5 specific items (not generic): - Strengths: what gives us an unfair advantage? - Weaknesses: what's holding us back right now? - Opportunities: what market shifts can we capitalize on in the next 12 months? - Threats: what could seriously hurt us? Then provide: - Top 3 strategic priorities based on this analysis - The single biggest risk to address immediately - The single biggest opportunity to pursue immediately Be brutally honest, not encouraging. I need truth, not motivation.
PROMPT 14
Revenue Stream Brainstorm
I run [YOUR BUSINESS]. Help me identify additional revenue streams. Current revenue: [HOW YOU MAKE MONEY NOW] Audience: [WHO YOUR CUSTOMERS ARE] Skills/assets: [WHAT YOU'RE GOOD AT, WHAT YOU HAVE] Available time: [HOW MUCH EXTRA TIME] Generate 10 revenue stream ideas, ranked by: - Effort to launch (Low/Medium/High) - Time to first dollar (Days/Weeks/Months) - Revenue potential ($/month estimate) - Fit with existing brand (1-10) Format as a table. Then deep-dive on the top 3 with specific first steps. Constraints: no MLM, no dropshipping, no "start a podcast" unless it directly serves the business.
PROMPT 15
Partnership Outreach Email
Write 3 cold outreach emails to propose a partnership between my business [YOUR BUSINESS] and [TYPE OF PARTNER - influencer, complementary brand, supplier]. My business: [WHAT YOU DO] Their business: [WHAT THEY DO] Proposed collaboration: [WHAT YOU'RE SUGGESTING] What's in it for them: [THEIR BENEFIT] Version 1: Ultra-short (under 75 words, mobile-optimized) Version 2: Value-first (lead with what you'll do for them) Version 3: Mutual benefit (frame as a joint opportunity) Rules: - No "I hope this email finds you well" - No "I'd love to pick your brain" - First line must be specific to THEM (not about you) - CTA is a specific low-commitment ask (not "let's hop on a call")
PROMPT 16
Pitch Deck Outline
Create a 10-slide pitch deck outline for [YOUR BUSINESS] seeking [INVESTMENT / PARTNERSHIP / CLIENT]. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Ask: [WHAT YOU WANT - funding amount, partnership terms, contract] Traction: [ANY NUMBERS - revenue, users, growth rate] Team: [KEY TEAM MEMBERS AND STRENGTHS] Slides: 1. Cover (company name + one-line value prop) 2. Problem (make them feel the pain) 3. Solution (your product, shown simply) 4. Market size (TAM/SAM/SOM) 5. Business model (how you make money) 6. Traction (proof it's working) 7. Competition (positioning matrix) 8. Team (why YOU can win) 9. Financials (projections, key metrics) 10. Ask (specific request + use of funds) For each slide: the key message (1 sentence), what visual to use, and what NOT to include. The best pitch decks have more images than words.

Operations & Efficiency (17-24)

PROMPT 17
SOP Generator
Create a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [YOUR PROCESS] at [YOUR BUSINESS]. Process: [DESCRIBE THE TASK - e.g., "processing a customer refund" or "onboarding a new client"] Who performs it: [ROLE] Frequency: [how often] Tools used: [SOFTWARE/PLATFORMS] Write: 1. Process title and purpose (1 sentence) 2. Numbered step-by-step instructions (a new employee should be able to follow this with zero training) 3. Decision points (if X, do Y; if Z, do W) 4. Common mistakes and how to avoid them 5. Quality check before marking complete 6. Estimated time to complete 7. Escalation: when to ask for help and who to ask Keep language simple. No jargon. Use "you" not "the employee."
PROMPT 18
Customer Support Response Templates
Write 10 customer support email templates for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Business: [WHAT YOU SELL] Tone: [friendly-professional / casual / formal] Create templates for: 1. Order confirmation 2. Shipping delay notification 3. Refund approved 4. Refund denied (with reason) 5. Product exchange instructions 6. Response to negative review/complaint 7. Follow-up after purchase (asking for review) 8. Out-of-stock notification with alternative 9. Loyalty discount for repeat customer 10. Response to "is this product right for me?" inquiry Each template: subject line + body under 100 words + [PERSONALIZATION BRACKETS] for custom details. The tone should feel human, not robotic.
PROMPT 19
Hiring: Job Description Writer
Write a job description for [ROLE] at [YOUR COMPANY]. Role: [JOB TITLE] Type: [full-time / part-time / contract / freelance] Location: [remote / on-site / hybrid] Salary range: [RANGE] Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY] Include: 1. Opening hook (2 sentences that make the RIGHT person excited, not everyone) 2. What you'll do (5-7 responsibilities, action verbs, specific not vague) 3. What you bring (requirements: split into "must have" and "nice to have") 4. What we offer (benefits, culture, growth — be specific) 5. How to apply (make it easy) Avoid: "fast-paced environment," "rockstar," "wear many hats," "competitive salary" without numbers, and any requirements that aren't actually required.
PROMPT 20
Meeting Agenda Generator
Create a meeting agenda for a [TYPE OF MEETING] at [YOUR COMPANY]. Meeting type: [weekly standup / quarterly review / brainstorm / client kickoff / board meeting] Duration: [30 min / 60 min / 90 min] Attendees: [WHO'S IN THE ROOM] Goal: [THE ONE THING THIS MEETING MUST ACCOMPLISH] Provide: 1. Pre-meeting prep (what attendees should do/read before) 2. Timed agenda items (topic, owner, duration, goal) 3. Decision items vs. discussion items (clearly labeled) 4. Parking lot protocol (how to handle tangents) 5. Post-meeting deliverables (who does what by when) Rule: if a meeting doesn't have a clear decision to make or action to assign, it should be an email. Flag if this meeting might be unnecessary.
PROMPT 21
Expense Tracker Categories
Set up a complete expense tracking system for my [YOUR TYPE] business. Business type: [freelancer / e-commerce / SaaS / agency / restaurant / etc.] Country: [FOR TAX PURPOSES] Annual revenue estimate: [RANGE] Create: 1. Complete list of expense categories relevant to my business type 2. Which categories are tax-deductible and how to maximize deductions 3. Monthly fixed vs. variable expense template 4. Red flag ratios (e.g., "if marketing exceeds X% of revenue, investigate") 5. Quarterly review checklist 6. What receipts/documentation to keep for each category Format the categories as a spreadsheet-ready list with columns: Category, Subcategory, Tax Deductible (Y/N), Typical Monthly Range, Notes.
PROMPT 22
Client Onboarding Checklist
Create a client onboarding checklist for [YOUR SERVICE BUSINESS]. Service: [WHAT YOU DO FOR CLIENTS] Typical project length: [DURATION] Tools: [PROJECT MANAGEMENT / COMMUNICATION TOOLS] Build a checklist covering: 1. Pre-onboarding (before they sign) 2. Day 1 (welcome + access + expectations) 3. Week 1 (kickoff meeting + initial deliverables) 4. Ongoing (check-in schedule + reporting cadence) For each step: action item, owner (you or client), timeline, and email/message template where applicable. Goal: make the client feel professionally handled from minute one. First impressions in services determine whether they refer you or ghost you.
PROMPT 23
FAQ Page Writer
Write a comprehensive FAQ page for [YOUR BUSINESS WEBSITE]. Business: [WHAT YOU SELL] Common customer questions: [LIST ANY YOU ALREADY KNOW] Main objections to buying: [WHY PEOPLE HESITATE] Write 15 FAQs covering: - Product/service questions (5) - Shipping/delivery/timeline questions (3) - Pricing/payment questions (3) - Trust/credibility questions (2) - Post-purchase/support questions (2) Each answer: 2-3 sentences max. Use the answer to subtly sell (address the objection, then pivot to a benefit). Include internal links to [relevant pages] where applicable. SEO note: phrase questions exactly how a real person would Google them.
PROMPT 24
Automation Opportunities Finder
Analyze my business operations and find automation opportunities. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Current manual tasks: [LIST YOUR REPETITIVE TASKS] Tools already using: [CURRENT SOFTWARE STACK] Budget for tools: [MONTHLY BUDGET] For each automation opportunity: 1. The manual task being replaced 2. Recommended tool (free options first, then paid) 3. Setup time estimate 4. Time saved per week 5. Cost vs. time-saved ROI Prioritize by: highest time savings with lowest setup effort. Focus on: Zapier/Make.com integrations, email automations, social media scheduling, invoice generation, lead capture, review requests, appointment booking. Skip anything that requires a developer to set up.

Content & SEO (25-30)

PROMPT 25
Blog Post SEO Framework
Write an SEO-optimized blog post outline for [YOUR BUSINESS BLOG]. Target keyword: [YOUR KEYWORD] Search intent: [informational / commercial / transactional] Word count target: [1500 / 2000 / 2500] Audience: [WHO'S SEARCHING THIS] Provide: 1. SEO title (under 60 characters, keyword front-loaded) 2. Meta description (under 155 characters, includes CTA) 3. H1 heading 4. Outline with H2 and H3 headers (structured for featured snippet) 5. 5 internal link opportunities [describe what pages to link to] 6. 3 external link suggestions (authoritative sources) 7. Image alt text suggestions (3 images) 8. Schema markup type recommendation Write the intro paragraph (hook + context + what they'll learn) and the conclusion (summary + CTA). The outline should answer the searcher's question within the first 200 words (for featured snippet), then go deeper.
PROMPT 26
YouTube Video Script
Write a YouTube video script for [YOUR CHANNEL/BUSINESS]. Topic: [VIDEO TOPIC] Length: [5 / 10 / 15 minutes] Audience: [WHO'S WATCHING] Goal: [educate / sell / build trust / entertain] Structure: 1. Hook (first 5 seconds — the line that prevents clicking away) 2. Context (why this matters, 15 seconds) 3. Credibility (why you should listen to me, 10 seconds) 4. Content delivery (main points with transitions) 5. CTA (subscribe, link in description, etc.) Include: - Timestamp markers for chapters - B-roll suggestions [describe what to show on screen] - On-screen text suggestions - Thumbnail concept (text + visual description) Write conversationally. Short sentences. One idea per sentence. This is spoken word, not an essay.
PROMPT 27
Keyword Research Assistant
Help me find keywords to target for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Location: [LOCAL OR NATIONAL] Current website authority: [new site / established / unknown] Generate: 1. 10 high-intent commercial keywords (people ready to buy) 2. 10 informational keywords (people researching) 3. 5 long-tail keywords with low competition (easy wins for new sites) 4. 5 question-based keywords (for FAQ/blog content) 5. 3 "comparison" keywords (e.g., "[your product] vs [competitor]") For each keyword estimate: - Search volume bracket (low/medium/high) - Competition level (easy/moderate/hard) - Content type needed (blog post, landing page, product page, video) Prioritize: what should I create content for FIRST to get the fastest traffic?
PROMPT 28
Newsletter Content Ideas (12 months)
Generate 52 weekly newsletter topic ideas for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Audience: [YOUR SUBSCRIBERS] Newsletter purpose: [nurture leads / drive sales / build community / establish authority] Organize by month with themes: - January: [theme] - February: [theme] ... etc. For each week provide: - Subject line (curiosity-driven, under 45 characters) - One-sentence topic description - Content type: story / tip / case study / opinion / curated links / behind-the-scenes Rules: - No more than 1 direct sales pitch per month - Every newsletter must give value even if they never buy - Include 4 "personal story" issues to build connection - Include 4 "controversial take" issues to drive engagement
PROMPT 29
Case Study Writer
Write a customer case study for [YOUR BUSINESS]. Client: [CLIENT NAME/INDUSTRY] Their problem: [WHAT THEY STRUGGLED WITH BEFORE] Your solution: [WHAT YOU DID FOR THEM] Results: [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES - numbers, percentages, time saved] Format: 1. Headline: "[Result] — How [Client] achieved [outcome] with [Your Product]" 2. The Challenge (3-4 sentences, make the reader feel the pain) 3. The Solution (what you did, step by step, 3-4 sentences) 4. The Results (specific metrics, before/after comparison) 5. Client quote (write a realistic testimonial they'd actually say) 6. Key takeaway (1 sentence the reader remembers) Keep it under 500 words. Use numbers in every section. Nobody reads case studies without numbers.
PROMPT 30
Lead Magnet Creator
Design a lead magnet for [YOUR BUSINESS] to capture email addresses. Business: [WHAT YOU SELL] Target audience: [WHO YOU WANT ON YOUR LIST] Goal: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO BUY AFTER] Suggest 5 lead magnet ideas, then deep-dive on the best one: - Title (benefit-driven, specific, uses a number) - Format (PDF checklist / template / mini-course / quiz / calculator) - Outline (what's inside, page by page) - Landing page headline and subheadline - 3 bullet points for the opt-in form - Delivery: immediate download vs. email sequence The lead magnet must: - Solve ONE specific problem completely (not partially) - Take under 10 minutes to consume - Make them think "if the free stuff is this good, the paid stuff must be amazing" - Naturally lead into your paid offering
