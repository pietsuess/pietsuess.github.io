Personal Finance Mastery

30 ChatGPT Power Prompts for Budgeting, Investing & Financial Freedom — Copy, paste, customize the [BRACKETED] parts.

Budgeting & Cash Flow (1-8)

PROMPT 01
Zero-Based Budget Builder
Create a zero-based monthly budget. Income: [MONTHLY TAKE-HOME]. Fixed expenses: [LIST]. Variable categories: [LIST]. Allocate every dollar. Flag any category over 30% of income. Output as a table with categories, budgeted amount, percentage of income.
Zero-based budgeting means every dollar has a job — nothing left "unassigned."
PROMPT 02
Expense Audit & Cut List
Analyze my monthly spending: [PASTE BANK STATEMENT SUMMARY OR LIST EXPENSES]. Identify: subscriptions I might not need, expenses that could be reduced by switching providers, lifestyle costs with the biggest savings potential. Rank cuts by annual savings impact. Be honest, not judgmental.
Paste a real month of spending for the most actionable results.
PROMPT 03
Emergency Fund Calculator
Calculate my emergency fund target. Monthly essentials (rent, food, insurance, minimum debt payments): [AMOUNT]. Job stability: [stable/moderate/freelance]. Dependents: [NUMBER]. Calculate 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month targets. Create a savings timeline based on saving [AMOUNT/MONTH].
Freelancers and those with dependents should aim for the higher end.
PROMPT 04
Debt Payoff Strategy
Create a debt payoff plan. My debts: [LIST EACH: name, balance, interest rate, minimum payment]. Extra monthly payment available: [AMOUNT]. Compare avalanche (highest interest first) vs. snowball (smallest balance first). Show me month-by-month payoff schedule, total interest paid, and payoff date for each method.
Avalanche saves the most money. Snowball builds momentum. Pick what keeps you going.
PROMPT 05
Subscription Audit Template
I currently pay for these subscriptions: [LIST ALL SUBSCRIPTIONS WITH MONTHLY COST]. Categorize each as: essential / nice-to-have / probably-wasting-money. Calculate annual cost of each category. Suggest free alternatives for the "wasting money" ones. Calculate total annual savings if I cut everything non-essential.
Most people underestimate their subscriptions by 30-40%. List everything.
PROMPT 06
50/30/20 Budget Optimizer
My monthly take-home is [AMOUNT]. Apply the 50/30/20 rule (needs/wants/savings). My current spending: [LIST MAJOR CATEGORIES AND AMOUNTS]. Show me where I'm over/under in each bucket. Suggest specific adjustments to hit the targets without making life miserable.
The 50/30/20 rule is a starting framework — adjust the ratios to fit your goals.
PROMPT 07
Irregular Expense Planner
Help me plan for irregular/annual expenses. List: [CAR INSURANCE, HOLIDAYS, BIRTHDAYS, HOME MAINTENANCE, etc. with approximate amounts]. Calculate the total annual cost, divide by 12, and create a monthly "sinking fund" savings plan so these expenses never surprise me.
Sinking funds turn financial emergencies into line items. Game changer.
PROMPT 08
Cash Flow Forecast
Create a 6-month cash flow forecast. Monthly income: [AMOUNT, note if variable]. Fixed monthly costs: [LIST]. Known upcoming expenses: [LIST WITH MONTHS]. Expected income changes: [ANY]. Show me month-by-month projected balance and flag any months where I might go negative.
If your income varies, use your lowest recent month as the baseline.

Investing & Wealth Building (9-16)

PROMPT 09
Beginner Investment Plan
I'm new to investing. Age: [AGE]. Risk tolerance: [conservative/moderate/aggressive]. Monthly amount to invest: [AMOUNT]. Time horizon: [YEARS]. Current knowledge level: [none/basic/intermediate]. Explain: where to open an account, what to buy first (index funds, ETFs), how to automate it, and what NOT to do. Keep it simple — no jargon.
The best time to start investing was 10 years ago. The second best is today.
PROMPT 10
Portfolio Allocation Review
Review my investment portfolio: [LIST HOLDINGS WITH PERCENTAGES]. Age: [AGE]. Goal: [RETIREMENT/HOUSE/etc]. Time horizon: [YEARS]. Risk tolerance: [LEVEL]. Is my allocation appropriate? Am I over-concentrated anywhere? Suggest rebalancing moves. Compare to a standard target-date allocation for my age.
Rebalancing once or twice a year is usually enough — don't overtrade.
PROMPT 11
Compound Interest Scenarios
Run compound interest projections. Starting amount: [AMOUNT]. Monthly contribution: [AMOUNT]. Expected annual return: [PERCENTAGE]. Time periods: 5, 10, 20, 30 years. Show me: total contributed vs. total growth, final balance at each milestone. Then show what happens if I increase my monthly contribution by just $50, $100, $200.
The "just $50 more" comparison is where the magic of compounding clicks.
PROMPT 12
TFSA/RRSP/401k Strategy
I'm [AGE] in [COUNTRY]. Income: [AMOUNT]. Available tax-advantaged accounts: [LIST: TFSA, RRSP, 401k, IRA, etc.]. Current balances: [AMOUNTS]. How should I prioritize contributions? Factor in employer matching, tax brackets, and withdrawal rules. Create a contribution strategy for this year.
Always capture employer matching first — it's free money.
PROMPT 13
Index Fund Comparison
Compare these index funds/ETFs for a long-term investor: [LIST 3-5 TICKERS]. Compare: expense ratio, holdings overlap, historical performance, dividend yield, tax efficiency. Which one(s) should I choose and why? I want to keep it simple — ideally 2-3 funds maximum.
Expense ratios matter more than most people realize over 20+ years.
PROMPT 14
Real Estate vs. Stock Market
Help me decide: should I save for a rental property or keep investing in the stock market? Current savings: [AMOUNT]. Monthly savings rate: [AMOUNT]. Local property prices: [RANGE]. Rental income potential: [ESTIMATE]. Compare 10-year projected returns for both paths, including all costs (maintenance, vacancy, property tax, management fees vs. capital gains tax, fees).
Most people underestimate the hidden costs of being a landlord.
PROMPT 15
Dividend Income Calculator
I want to build a dividend income stream. Target monthly passive income: [AMOUNT]. Current portfolio: [AMOUNT]. Average dividend yield target: [3-5%]. Calculate: how much I need invested to hit my target, how long it will take at [MONTHLY CONTRIBUTION], and suggest a diversified dividend strategy (not just chasing highest yields).
High yields can signal risk. A balanced approach beats yield-chasing every time.
PROMPT 16
Dollar-Cost Averaging Plan
Create a dollar-cost averaging plan. Asset to buy: [ETF/INDEX FUND]. Monthly amount: [AMOUNT]. Explain why DCA works, set up a specific schedule (which day of month, auto-invest settings), and show a historical simulation of DCA vs. lump sum for this asset over the last 10 years.
DCA removes emotion from investing — you buy the dips automatically.

Tax Optimization (17-22)

PROMPT 17
Tax Deduction Finder
I'm a [EMPLOYEE/FREELANCER/BUSINESS OWNER] in [COUNTRY/STATE]. Income: [AMOUNT]. Situation: [HOMEOWNER/RENTER, MARRIED/SINGLE, KIDS/NO KIDS]. List every tax deduction and credit I might be eligible for. Estimate the tax savings for each. Highlight anything I'm probably missing.
Most people leave money on the table because they don't know what to claim.
PROMPT 18
Freelancer Tax Prep
I'm a freelancer. Revenue this year: [AMOUNT]. Business expenses: [LIST MAJOR CATEGORIES]. Home office: [YES/NO, SQUARE FOOTAGE]. Vehicle use: [BUSINESS MILES]. Help me: organize expenses by tax category, calculate estimated quarterly payments, list deductions I might be forgetting, and create a year-end tax prep checklist.
Track expenses year-round — scrambling in April costs you deductions.
PROMPT 19
Tax-Loss Harvesting Guide
My taxable investment account has these positions: [LIST WITH CURRENT GAIN/LOSS]. Explain tax-loss harvesting for my specific situation. Which positions should I consider selling? What are the wash sale rules? How much could I save in taxes? What should I buy as replacements?
Tax-loss harvesting is one of the few guaranteed ways to reduce your tax bill on investments.
PROMPT 20
Year-End Tax Checklist
It's [MONTH]. Create a year-end tax optimization checklist for my situation: Income: [AMOUNT]. Filing status: [STATUS]. Retirement account contributions so far: [AMOUNTS]. Charitable giving: [AMOUNT]. What moves should I make before December 31 to minimize my tax bill?
The best time to do year-end tax planning is October, not December.
PROMPT 21
Capital Gains Strategy
I want to sell [INVESTMENT] with a [GAIN/LOSS] of [AMOUNT]. Purchase date: [DATE]. My income this year: [AMOUNT]. Tax filing status: [STATUS]. Calculate my capital gains tax (short-term vs. long-term). Should I sell now or wait? Are there strategies to reduce the tax hit?
Holding 1 day past the 1-year mark can cut your tax rate significantly.
PROMPT 22
Side Income Tax Guide
I earned [AMOUNT] in side income from [SOURCE: freelancing, selling online, crypto, rental income, etc.]. My day job income: [AMOUNT]. Do I need to report this? What's my tax obligation? Do I need to make quarterly payments? What expenses can I deduct? Walk me through it simply.
Yes, you almost certainly need to report it. This prompt helps you do it right.

Financial Goals & Planning (23-30)

PROMPT 23
Net Worth Tracker
Calculate my current net worth. Assets: [LIST: savings, investments, property, vehicles, other]. Liabilities: [LIST: mortgage, student loans, credit cards, car loans, other]. Show me: total assets, total liabilities, net worth. Then set 1-year and 5-year net worth targets with an action plan to hit them.
Net worth is the only number that tells the full financial picture.
PROMPT 24
House Buying Readiness
Am I ready to buy a house? Income: [AMOUNT]. Savings: [AMOUNT]. Monthly debts: [AMOUNT]. Credit score: [SCORE]. Target home price: [RANGE]. Location: [CITY/AREA]. Calculate: maximum mortgage I qualify for, required down payment, monthly payment (PITI), how much more I need to save, and a timeline to be ready.
The purchase price is just the start — factor in closing costs, maintenance, and insurance.
PROMPT 25
Financial Independence Calculator
Calculate my path to financial independence / early retirement. Current age: [AGE]. Target retirement age: [AGE]. Annual expenses: [AMOUNT]. Current investments: [AMOUNT]. Monthly savings: [AMOUNT]. Expected return: [%]. Using the 4% rule, calculate: my FI number, projected timeline, and what changes would accelerate it (cutting expenses by 10%, increasing income by 20%, etc.).
The 4% rule says you need 25x your annual expenses invested to retire.
PROMPT 26
Insurance Coverage Review
Review my insurance coverage. Current policies: [LIST: health, auto, home/renters, life, disability, umbrella, with coverage amounts and premiums]. Age: [AGE]. Dependents: [NUMBER]. Assets: [TOTAL]. Am I over-insured or under-insured anywhere? What gaps should I fill? Where can I save money?
Insurance is about protecting against catastrophe, not small inconveniences.
PROMPT 27
Financial Conversation Scripts
I need to have a money conversation with [PARTNER/PARENT/ADULT CHILD/ROOMMATE] about [TOPIC: splitting expenses, lending money, inheritance planning, spending habits, financial goals]. Give me: a script for opening the conversation without triggering defensiveness, key questions to ask, how to handle pushback, and how to reach a concrete agreement.
Money conversations are relationship conversations. The script matters.
PROMPT 28
Savings Goal Sprint
I need to save [AMOUNT] by [DATE] for [PURPOSE]. Current savings toward this goal: [AMOUNT]. Monthly income: [AMOUNT]. Monthly expenses: [AMOUNT]. Create an aggressive but realistic savings sprint plan. Include: weekly savings targets, specific expenses to cut temporarily, ways to earn extra income this month, and progress milestones to keep me motivated.
Short-term sprints work because they have a clear finish line.
PROMPT 29
Credit Score Improvement Plan
My credit score is [SCORE]. I want to reach [TARGET SCORE] by [DATE]. Current credit cards: [LIST WITH BALANCES AND LIMITS]. Any negative marks: [LATE PAYMENTS, COLLECTIONS, etc.]. Create a month-by-month action plan to improve my score. Explain which factors matter most and which actions have the fastest impact.
Utilization ratio (keeping balances under 30% of limits) is the fastest lever to pull.
PROMPT 30
Annual Financial Review
Conduct my annual financial review. This year: Income [AMOUNT], Spending [AMOUNT], Saved [AMOUNT], Invested [AMOUNT]. Debt change: [INCREASED/DECREASED BY AMOUNT]. Net worth change: [AMOUNT]. Rate my financial health (A-F) in: savings rate, debt management, investment growth, emergency preparedness, insurance coverage. Set 3 specific financial goals for next year with quarterly milestones.
An annual review turns financial awareness into financial progress.
