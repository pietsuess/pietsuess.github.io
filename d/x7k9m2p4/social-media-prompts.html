Social Media Content Machine: 40 ChatGPT Prompts

For ChatGPT, Claude, or any LLM — Copy, paste, post. Never stare at a blank screen again.

Instagram Captions & Hooks

PROMPT 01
Scroll-Stopping Hooks Generator
You are an Instagram copywriter who specializes in writing opening hooks that stop the scroll. My niche is [YOUR NICHE] and my target audience is [YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]. Generate 10 unique opening hook lines for Instagram captions. Each hook should be no longer than one sentence and must create an immediate emotional reaction — curiosity, surprise, urgency, or disagreement. For each hook, include a brief note in parentheses explaining the psychological trigger it uses (e.g., curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, bold claim, direct address). Avoid cliches like "Did you know?" or "Here's the thing." Make them feel like something a real person would say out loud, not a brand.
Tip: Save the best 3 hooks and test them as Reels cover text or Story openers to see which get the most replies.
PROMPT 02
Carousel Slide-by-Slide Copy
Write a complete 10-slide Instagram carousel post about [YOUR TOPIC]. My brand is [YOUR BRAND] in the [YOUR NICHE] space, and the goal is to educate my audience while positioning me as an authority. Structure it exactly like this: Slide 1 — A bold, curiosity-driven headline (max 8 words, designed to make people swipe). Slides 2-9 — One clear point per slide. Each slide should have a short heading (3-6 words, bold and punchy) and 1-2 supporting sentences underneath. Keep language conversational and direct. Use concrete examples or numbers where possible. Slide 10 — A strong CTA that tells the reader exactly what to do next (follow, save, share, comment, or visit link in bio). Also write the caption to go underneath the carousel: it should be 3-4 sentences max, include 1 question to encourage comments, and end with a set of 5 relevant niche hashtags.
PROMPT 03
Before/After Transformation Caption
Write an Instagram caption that tells a compelling before-and-after transformation story for [YOUR BRAND/PRODUCT/SERVICE]. The caption should follow this structure: Start with a vivid, specific description of the "before" state — what was the problem, frustration, or struggle? Use sensory details and emotional language so the reader can feel it. Then transition with a turning-point sentence (the moment everything changed). Describe the "after" state with specific, measurable results or emotional outcomes. Close with a 1-2 sentence CTA that invites the reader to experience their own transformation. Keep the total length between 150-200 words. Tone should be authentic and conversational, not salesy. Include 3 relevant emojis placed naturally throughout the text and suggest 8 hashtags at the end — a mix of broad (500K+ posts), medium (50K-500K), and niche (under 50K).
Tip: Pair this caption with a simple side-by-side image or a "then vs. now" photo for maximum impact.
PROMPT 04
Story-Driven Caption with CTA
Write an Instagram caption that tells a real, personal-feeling story to promote [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE/OFFER]. The story should follow the classic narrative arc: Set the scene in one sentence (where, when, what was happening). Introduce a problem or tension that your audience can deeply relate to. Describe the moment of realization or discovery that led to a shift. Reveal the outcome and connect it naturally to what you offer — without it feeling like a hard pitch. End with a clear, specific CTA (not just "link in bio" — tell them exactly what they'll get when they click). The tone should sound like you're texting a close friend, not writing ad copy. Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each) with line breaks between them for easy mobile reading. Total length: 180-250 words.
PROMPT 05
Strategic Hashtag Generator
Act as an Instagram growth strategist. I run a [YOUR NICHE] account with [YOUR FOLLOWER COUNT] followers. My target audience is [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL FOLLOWER]. Generate 4 separate hashtag sets I can rotate between posts. Each set should contain exactly 20 hashtags organized into three tiers: 5 large hashtags (1M+ posts — for reach), 8 medium hashtags (100K-1M posts — for discoverability), and 7 small/niche hashtags (under 100K posts — for ranking). For each set, include a one-line note explaining what type of content that set works best for (e.g., educational carousels, personal stories, product showcases, behind-the-scenes). Also list 5 hashtags I should absolutely AVOID in my niche because they attract spam, bots, or the wrong audience.
Tip: Rotate these sets across your posts. Never use the same 20 hashtags twice in a row — Instagram may flag it as spammy behavior.
PROMPT 06
Controversial Take / Hot Take Post
Write 5 "controversial opinion" Instagram captions for a [YOUR NICHE] brand. Each post should open with a bold, slightly polarizing statement that challenges a common belief in the industry — something that will make people stop scrolling and either nod vigorously or feel compelled to argue in the comments. After the opening statement, include 3-4 sentences that back up the opinion with logic, experience, or evidence. The tone should be confident and direct but not arrogant — you're sharing a perspective, not attacking anyone. End each caption with a question that invites people to share their own take (this drives comment engagement). Keep each caption under 150 words. Important: these should be genuinely thought-provoking opinions, not rage bait. The goal is respectful debate that builds authority, not controversy for its own sake.
PROMPT 07
"Save This" Educational Post
Create an Instagram caption designed to get saves and shares. The topic is [YOUR TOPIC] for [YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]. Structure the caption as a mini-tutorial or cheat sheet that people will want to bookmark for later. Start with a hook line like "Save this for next time you [RELEVANT SITUATION]." Then deliver 5-7 actionable tips, tricks, or steps — each as a numbered item with a brief explanation (1-2 sentences max per item). Use specific, practical advice they can implement immediately, not vague generalities. After the list, add a 1-sentence CTA asking readers to save the post and tag someone who needs it. Format with line breaks between each numbered point for readability. Total length: 200-300 words. End with 5 highly relevant niche hashtags and one branded hashtag for [YOUR BRAND].
Tip: Posts that get high save rates are heavily favored by the Instagram algorithm. Pair this caption with a clean, text-based graphic for the feed image.
PROMPT 08
Behind-the-Scenes Content Caption
Write 5 Instagram caption options for behind-the-scenes content from my [YOUR NICHE] business. Each caption should give followers a peek behind the curtain while reinforcing brand trust and relatability. For each one, use a different BTS angle: (1) the messy process behind a polished final product, (2) a day-in-the-life snapshot with honest details, (3) a mistake or failure you learned from, (4) a team member or collaborator spotlight, and (5) the "why" behind a recent business decision. Each caption should be 80-120 words, start with a hook that makes the reader curious about what they're seeing, and end with a question that invites the audience to share their own experience. Tone: warm, honest, slightly vulnerable — like letting someone into your workshop for the first time.
PROMPT 09
Product Launch Caption Suite
I'm launching [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] on [LAUNCH DATE]. Write a sequence of 5 Instagram captions to build anticipation and drive sales across launch week. Caption 1 (5 days before): A teaser that hints at something coming without revealing what it is. Build curiosity and tell followers to turn on notifications. Caption 2 (2 days before): Reveal what it is, share the core benefit, and explain who it's for. Include a waitlist or early-access CTA. Caption 3 (Launch day): The main launch announcement. Lead with the biggest transformation or result your product delivers. Include clear purchase instructions and urgency (limited spots, early bird pricing, bonus expires). Caption 4 (Day 2): Social proof and testimonials focus — use a quote or story from a beta user or early customer. Caption 5 (Final day): Last-chance urgency post. Remind them what they're missing and when the offer closes. Each caption should be 100-180 words with a strong CTA. Tone: excited and confident, not desperate.
Tip: Schedule these in advance using a tool like Later or Buffer so you can focus on engaging with comments during launch week.
PROMPT 10
Community Engagement Questions
Generate 15 Instagram engagement questions for a [YOUR NICHE] account that will genuinely make people want to respond in the comments. Organize them into three categories: (A) "This or That" — 5 fun either/or questions relevant to your niche that are easy to answer in one word (these drive high comment volume). (B) "Storytime" — 5 open-ended questions that invite followers to share a personal experience or opinion related to [YOUR NICHE] (these drive longer, more meaningful conversations). (C) "Advice Seeking" — 5 questions where you ask your audience for their expertise or recommendation (these make followers feel valued and boost loyalty). For each question, include a 1-sentence caption setup that gives context before the question. Also suggest whether each one works best as a feed post caption, a Story poll, or a Reel talking-head prompt.

TikTok & Reels Scripts

PROMPT 11
"3 Things" Listicle Script
Write a 60-second TikTok/Reels script using the proven "3 Things" format. The topic is: 3 things [YOUR AUDIENCE] should know about [YOUR TOPIC]. Structure the script exactly like this: HOOK (0-3 seconds): A bold, direct statement or question that creates an immediate curiosity gap. Must reference the number 3 and the topic. POINT 1 (3-18 seconds): State the first thing clearly in one sentence, then give a brief explanation or example in 2-3 sentences. Use specific details, not generalities. POINT 2 (18-35 seconds): Same structure. This should be the most surprising or counterintuitive point. POINT 3 (35-50 seconds): Same structure. Save the most actionable or valuable point for last. CTA (50-60 seconds): Tell viewers exactly what to do — follow for more, comment which one surprised them most, or save for later. Include on-screen text suggestions for each section in [brackets]. Keep the language punchy and conversational — write it like you're talking to one person across a table, not reading from a teleprompter.
Tip: Film each point as a separate clip with a quick cut between them. Jump cuts keep attention on short-form video.
PROMPT 12
Viewer Retention Hook Scripts
Write 8 TikTok/Reels opening hooks (the first 3 seconds) that are designed to stop viewers from swiping away. My niche is [YOUR NICHE] and my content focus is [YOUR CONTENT FOCUS]. Each hook should use a different psychological retention technique: (1) Bold claim that demands proof, (2) Unfinished story that needs resolution, (3) Visual mismatch setup ("this looks like X but it's actually..."), (4) Direct challenge to the viewer, (5) Shocking statistic or number, (6) "Don't do this" warning, (7) Social proof opener ("I've helped 200+ people do X"), (8) Pattern interrupt with unexpected first word. For each hook, write the exact spoken script (under 10 words) AND a note about what should be happening visually on screen during those 3 seconds. Also include a 2-sentence continuation for each hook so I know how to transition into the body of the video.
PROMPT 13
Trend-Jacking Script Template
I want to create a TikTok/Reel that uses the trending [DESCRIBE THE TREND — audio, format, or meme] and adapts it for my [YOUR NICHE] brand. Write a complete script that includes: The original trend format explained in one sentence (so I understand the template). My adapted version with specific dialogue or text overlay for each beat of the trend. Exact timing cues (e.g., "0-2s: show X, 2-5s: cut to Y"). A caption that connects the trend to my niche and includes a relevant CTA. 5 hashtags: 2 trend-specific, 2 niche-specific, and 1 general reach hashtag. Also write an alternative version of the same trend with a different angle in case the first doesn't feel right. The tone should match TikTok culture — self-aware, slightly irreverent, and genuine. Avoid anything that feels like it was written by a marketing department.
Tip: Speed matters with trends. Use this prompt the same day you spot a trend — waiting even 48 hours can mean you've missed the wave.
PROMPT 14
"Day in My Life" Narration Script
Write a voiceover narration script for a "day in my life" TikTok/Reel. I am a [YOUR JOB/ROLE] in the [YOUR INDUSTRY] space. The video should cover a realistic but aspirational day — the kind that makes viewers think "I want that life" while also being relatable and honest. Structure the script with timestamps for a 90-second video: Morning routine (0-20s) — 2-3 sentences with specific details (not generic "woke up and had coffee"). Work/hustle segment (20-50s) — show the actual interesting parts of what you do, mention a specific project or client type without naming names. Midday break or transition moment (50-65s) — something human and relatable. Afternoon/evening wind-down (65-85s) — how you decompress or shift gears. Closing thought (85-90s) — one reflective sentence that ties it back to your brand or mission. The narration tone should be calm, confident, and slightly poetic — think Casey Neistat meets a journal entry. Include suggestions for B-roll clips to film for each segment.
PROMPT 15
Step-by-Step Tutorial Script
Write a 45-60 second TikTok/Reels tutorial script that teaches [YOUR AUDIENCE] how to [SPECIFIC SKILL OR TASK]. The tutorial should follow this proven educational format: HOOK (0-3s): "Here's how to [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]" — promise a specific outcome immediately. STEP 1 (3-15s): The first action they need to take. Explain it simply in 1-2 sentences as if talking to a complete beginner. Include what to show on screen. STEP 2 (15-28s): The next action. Add one pro tip or common mistake to avoid. STEP 3 (28-40s): The final action that brings the result together. RESULT REVEAL (40-50s): Show or describe the finished outcome. Make it satisfying and concrete. CTA (50-60s): "Follow for more [TOPIC] tips" or "Save this for when you need it." Write the spoken narration separately from the on-screen text suggestions. Keep sentences short — max 12 words each for spoken delivery. The overall pace should feel brisk but not rushed.
Tip: Tutorials consistently outperform other content types for follower growth because they deliver immediate, tangible value.
PROMPT 16
"POV" Format Script
Write 3 TikTok/Reels scripts using the "POV" (point of view) format for my [YOUR NICHE] brand. Each POV should put the viewer in a specific, relatable scenario that your target audience ([YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]) has experienced. For each script include: The text overlay (starts with "POV:") — one sentence, max 15 words, that sets the scene instantly. A description of what the viewer should see on screen (your facial expressions, actions, setting). The audio approach — whether to use a trending sound, original voiceover, or no audio with just text. A suggested caption (2-3 sentences) that adds context or humor and includes a CTA. Keep each POV emotionally resonant — the best POV videos make the viewer feel genuinely seen. One should be funny/lighthearted, one should be aspirational/motivational, and one should be nostalgic or emotionally touching. Also write 2 "POV" concepts that would work specifically as a series (parts 1, 2, 3) to build recurring viewership.
PROMPT 17
Honest Product Review Script
Write a 60-second TikTok/Reels script for an authentic product review of [YOUR PRODUCT or a product in YOUR NICHE]. The review should feel genuine and trustworthy, not like a paid ad. Follow this structure: HOOK (0-5s): Start with your honest verdict in one sentence — don't bury the lead. Viewers will leave if they have to wait 50 seconds for your opinion. CONTEXT (5-15s): Briefly explain what the product is, who it's for, and how long you've been using it. Include one specific detail that shows you actually used it. PROS (15-30s): 2-3 specific things you liked. Use concrete examples ("the battery lasted 14 hours on a road trip" not "great battery life"). CONS (30-42s): 1-2 honest criticisms. This is what makes the review credible. WHO IT'S FOR (42-52s): Clearly state who should and shouldn't buy it. CTA (52-60s): Ask viewers if they've tried it, or tell them to follow for more honest reviews. Write the on-screen text overlays separately. Tone: like recommending something to a friend who asked.
Tip: Reviews that include at least one genuine criticism get significantly higher engagement and trust than purely positive ones.

This should be a 60-75 second video with 5 lessons. Structure: HOOK (0-4s): "5 things I wish someone told me before I [ACTIVITY]. Number 3 changed everything." — Open with the list promise and tease the best one to keep viewers watching. THING 1 (4-16s): The most universally relatable one. Start accessible. THING 2 (16-28s): Something slightly more specific to your experience. THING 3 (28-42s): The most impactful or surprising lesson — this is the one you teased in the hook. Give it extra detail and emotional weight. THING 4 (42-52s): A practical, tactical lesson. THING 5 (52-65s): The mindset shift that ties everything together. CTA (65-75s): "What would you add to this list? Drop it in the comments." Include on-screen text for each numbered point (5 words max per point) and write the full spoken narration separately. The tone should be reflective and generous — you're saving someone else from your mistakes.
PROMPT 19
Duet/Stitch Response Script
Write 4 TikTok Stitch/Duet response scripts for someone in the [YOUR NICHE] space. For each script, describe: The type of original video you should stitch or duet with (e.g., "a viral video where someone claims [COMMON MISCONCEPTION IN YOUR NICHE]" or "a trending question about [TOPIC]"). The first 2-3 seconds of the original clip to play before your response begins. Your response script (15-45 seconds) that either: adds expert context, respectfully disagrees with evidence, confirms and expands on their point, or takes their idea in a surprising new direction. Each response should position you as a knowledgeable, thoughtful voice in your niche — not as someone who picks fights for views. One should agree and build, one should politely disagree, one should add a "what they didn't mention" angle, and one should take a humorous approach.

LinkedIn Professional Content

PROMPT 21
Contrarian Take That Drives Comments
Write a LinkedIn post that challenges a widely accepted belief in the [YOUR INDUSTRY] space. The post should follow this structure: Line 1 — A bold, one-sentence contrarian statement that makes people stop scrolling. This should feel like a professional "hot take" that's backed by real thinking, not clickbait. Lines 2-4 — Acknowledge why most people believe the conventional wisdom. Show that you understand the mainstream position before you challenge it. Lines 5-10 — Present your alternative perspective with 2-3 concrete supporting points. Use specific examples, data, or firsthand experience. Avoid being vague — name frameworks, numbers, or real-world situations. Lines 11-12 — A nuanced closing that acknowledges your take isn't absolute. The best LinkedIn posts invite discussion, not argument. Final line — A genuine question asking for others' perspectives. Keep the total post under 200 words. Use line breaks between every 1-2 sentences for mobile readability. No hashtags — they reduce reach on LinkedIn in 2025+. Tone: thoughtful authority, not arrogance.
Tip: Posts that generate comments in the first 60 minutes get massively boosted by LinkedIn's algorithm. Reply to every comment within the first hour.
PROMPT 22
Failure-to-Lesson Storytelling Post
Write a LinkedIn post that tells a professional failure story and extracts a powerful lesson. I work in [YOUR INDUSTRY] as a [YOUR ROLE]. The story should follow this narrative arc: Opening hook (1 line): A vulnerable admission that creates immediate intrigue. Example format: "I [DID SOMETHING] and it [UNEXPECTED CONSEQUENCE]." The setup (3-4 lines): Paint the scene. What were you trying to do? What did you believe at the time? Include one specific detail that makes the story feel real (a date, a number, a name, a place). The failure (3-4 lines): What went wrong? Be specific and honest. Don't sugarcoat it. Readers can smell a fake failure story from a mile away. The lesson (3-4 lines): What did you learn? Frame it as a principle that applies to others in your field, not just your specific situation. The takeaway (1-2 lines): One sentence of actionable advice someone can apply today. Closing question: Invite others to share a similar experience. Total length: 150-200 words. Write in first person. Use short paragraphs. No bullet points — this should read like a story, not a listicle.
PROMPT 23
Data-Driven Insight Post
Write a LinkedIn post that presents a data-driven insight about [YOUR INDUSTRY/TOPIC]. The post should make one clear, specific argument supported by numbers. Structure: Hook line: Lead with the most surprising statistic or data point. Frame it as a contrast or contradiction (e.g., "X% of companies do [THING], but only Y% see results. Here's the gap nobody talks about."). Context (3-4 lines): Explain where this data comes from and why it matters right now. Don't just cite the stat — interpret what it means for the reader's daily work or business decisions. Analysis (4-6 lines): Break down the "why" behind the numbers. Offer 2-3 possible explanations. Reference your own professional experience to add credibility beyond the data itself. Implication (2-3 lines): What should the reader do differently based on this insight? Give one concrete, specific action step. Closing: Ask readers if the data matches their experience. Note: If you don't have real data, use this prompt to create a template with [STAT] placeholders I can fill in with actual numbers from recent industry reports.
Tip: Posts with specific numbers (percentages, dollar amounts, timeframes) consistently outperform vague observation posts on LinkedIn.
PROMPT 24
"We're Hiring" Post That Doesn't Sound Generic
Write a LinkedIn hiring post for a [JOB TITLE] role at [YOUR COMPANY] that actually makes talented people want to apply — not the usual corporate "We're hiring!" template. The post should: Open with why this role matters — what problem will this person solve, or what opportunity will they shape? In 2-3 sentences, describe what makes working here genuinely different. Be specific. "Great culture" means nothing. Mention something concrete: a project they'll work on, a decision they'll own, a skill they'll develop, a team ritual that's actually fun. Describe the ideal candidate in human terms — not a laundry list of requirements, but the kind of person who would thrive (their mindset, their working style, what excites them). Include the key practical details: location/remote, experience level, compensation range if possible. End with clear instructions on how to apply AND ask your network to share it with someone specific ("Tag someone who loves [X] and wants to [Y]"). Keep it under 200 words. Write it in the hiring manager's voice, not HR's voice.
PROMPT 25
Milestone/Achievement Announcement
Write a LinkedIn post announcing a professional milestone or achievement: [DESCRIBE YOUR MILESTONE — e.g., new role, company anniversary, award, revenue target, project launch]. The post should celebrate the win without being braggy. Follow this framework: Opening line: State the achievement simply and directly. No fake humility ("I'm humbled and honored...") — just say what happened. The journey paragraph (4-5 lines): Share what it actually took to get here. Mention a specific challenge, setback, or moment of doubt along the way. This is what makes the post relatable instead of insufferable. The gratitude paragraph (3-4 lines): Thank specific people or groups who helped — and be specific about what they did. "Thanks to my amazing team" is forgettable. "Thanks to [NAME] who stayed late every Thursday to help me figure out [SPECIFIC THING]" is memorable. The forward-looking line: One sentence about what comes next — what you're excited to build, learn, or tackle. Closing: A question that invites your network to share their own wins. Total: 150-180 words.
Tip: Milestone posts perform best when posted on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday mornings between 7-9 AM in your audience's time zone.
PROMPT 26
Industry Trend Analysis Post
Write a LinkedIn post analyzing a current trend in [YOUR INDUSTRY]: [DESCRIBE THE TREND]. Position yourself as a thoughtful voice who sees what's coming. Structure the post as follows: Hook (1 line): A prediction or observation that signals you're paying close attention to where the industry is heading. Use a specific timeframe: "In the next 12 months..." or "By end of 2026..." Observation (3-4 lines): Describe what you're seeing right now — specific signals, shifts in behavior, new tools or approaches gaining traction. Name names. Reference real companies, tools, or events where possible. Analysis (4-5 lines): Explain what's driving this trend and what it means for professionals in your field. Connect dots that others haven't connected yet. This is where your unique perspective and experience add value beyond what someone could Google. Prediction (2-3 lines): Make a specific, falsifiable prediction. "I think X will happen by Y" is more valuable than "things are changing." Action step (1-2 lines): What should someone do right now to prepare for or capitalize on this trend? Closing: Ask if others are seeing the same signals.
PROMPT 27
"Advice to My Younger Self" Post
Write a LinkedIn post in the "advice to my younger self" format. I am a [YOUR ROLE] with [X YEARS] of experience in [YOUR INDUSTRY]. Write the post as a letter or list of advice to yourself at the beginning of your career. Include 7 specific pieces of advice, each consisting of: A bold one-liner statement (the advice itself — direct and memorable). A 1-2 sentence expansion explaining why this matters, using a brief anecdote or specific example from real professional life. The advice should cover a range of themes: career strategy, skill development, relationships/networking, mindset, money/negotiation, work-life balance, and one unconventional insight specific to your industry. Tone: warm, reflective, slightly wistful — like a mentor talking to someone they care about. Avoid cliches like "bet on yourself" or "your network is your net worth" unless you can add a genuinely fresh spin. Open the post with: "If I could sit down with [YEAR] me for 10 minutes, I'd say this:" Close by asking your audience to share the one piece of advice they'd give their younger selves.
Tip: This format performs extremely well because it's simultaneously personal and universally applicable. It also positions you as someone with hard-won wisdom.
PROMPT 28
Case Study Breakdown Post
Write a LinkedIn post that breaks down a professional case study. The case study is about: [DESCRIBE THE PROJECT, RESULT, OR CLIENT SUCCESS STORY]. Structure the post using this framework: THE PROBLEM (3 lines): What was the specific challenge? Include a number or metric that quantifies the problem. Make the reader feel the pain. THE APPROACH (4-5 lines): What did you actually do? Be specific about the strategy, not vague. Mention the key decisions you made and why you chose that path over alternatives. Don't just say "we developed a strategy" — say what the strategy was. THE RESULT (2-3 lines): What happened? Lead with the most impressive metric. Include a before-and-after comparison if possible. Use exact numbers. THE LESSON (2-3 lines): What's the transferable insight? What can someone reading this post apply to their own work, even if they're in a different industry? Keep the total post under 200 words. Use bold formatting sparingly (LinkedIn supports it via Unicode characters) for the section headers. Don't name the client unless you have permission — describe them by industry and size instead.
PROMPT 29
"Unpopular Opinion" Post with Nuance
Write a LinkedIn "unpopular opinion" post about [YOUR TOPIC IN YOUR INDUSTRY]. The post should take a stance that goes against mainstream professional advice, but support it with nuanced reasoning — not just shock value. Structure: Line 1: "Unpopular opinion:" followed by your take in one clear sentence. Lines 2-3: Acknowledge the popular counterargument. Show you understand WHY most people disagree. This is critical — it separates a thoughtful contrarian from a troll. Lines 4-8: Present your case. Use 2-3 supporting arguments. Each one should draw from a different source of evidence: personal experience, observed patterns in the industry, and a logical argument or analogy. Lines 9-10: Add the nuance. Under what conditions is the popular opinion actually correct? When does your unpopular take NOT apply? This shows intellectual honesty and keeps the post from feeling one-dimensional. Final line: A question inviting civil debate. Example: "Curious whether you agree or I'm completely off base here. What's been your experience?" Tone: confident but genuinely open to being wrong. Length: 180-220 words max.
Tip: The best LinkedIn "unpopular opinion" posts are ones where you're genuinely uncertain. If you're 100% sure you're right, the post probably isn't interesting enough.
PROMPT 30
Networking Event Follow-Up Post
Write a LinkedIn post to share after attending [EVENT NAME], a [TYPE OF EVENT — conference, meetup, workshop, summit] for [INDUSTRY/TOPIC]. The post should accomplish three things: make new connections feel recognized, share a genuine takeaway, and position you as someone who shows up and engages with your community. Structure: Opening (2 lines): State where you just were and one word or phrase that captures the overall experience. Don't say "amazing" — choose something more specific and honest. Key takeaway (4-5 lines): Share the single most valuable insight you gained. Attribute it to the speaker or conversation where you heard it. Include enough detail that someone who wasn't there can benefit from it too. This is the part that provides value beyond just "I went to a thing." People spotlight (2-3 lines): Name 2-3 specific people you met or reconnected with and tag them. Mention something specific you discussed or plan to follow up on. This is the relationship-building power move. Forward-looking close (1-2 lines): What will you do differently based on what you learned? Invitation: Ask your network what events they'd recommend or if they were also there. Attach a photo if possible — LinkedIn heavily favors posts with native images.

Content Strategy & Planning

PROMPT 31
30-Day Content Calendar Generator
Create a complete 30-day social media content calendar for a [YOUR NICHE] brand. The primary platform is [PLATFORM] and the secondary platform is [PLATFORM]. My main business goals this month are: [GOAL 1] and [GOAL 2]. My audience is [DESCRIBE TARGET AUDIENCE]. For each of the 30 days, provide: The content type (carousel, Reel, static post, Story, text post, etc.). A specific topic or angle. A one-sentence caption concept or hook.
