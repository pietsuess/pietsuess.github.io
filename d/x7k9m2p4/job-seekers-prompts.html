Output the final resume in clean plain text, ready to paste into a document.

Tip: Paste the entire job posting, not just the title. ATS systems scan for exact keyword matches, so the more detail you give, the better the optimization.

4 Resume Gap Explainer
You are a career coach who specializes in helping candidates address employment gaps. I have a gap in my employment history and need help framing it positively. Gap Details: - Gap Period: [START DATE] to [END DATE] - Reason for Gap: [CAREGIVING / HEALTH ISSUE / LAYOFF / TRAVEL / PERSONAL PROJECT / EDUCATION / OTHER: describe] - What I did during the gap (if anything): [ANY FREELANCE WORK, VOLUNTEERING, COURSES, PERSONAL DEVELOPMENT, CAREGIVING RESPONSIBILITIES, ETC.] - Role I'm applying for now: [TARGET ROLE/INDUSTRY] Please provide: 1. A 2-3 sentence explanation I can include in my resume summary or cover letter that frames this gap honestly but positively. 2. 13 Cover Letter That Gets Read
You are a cover letter specialist. Write a cover letter that a hiring manager will actually read, for the following role: Job Title: [JOB TITLE] Company: [COMPANY NAME] Job Posting: """ [PASTE THE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE] """ My Background: - Current/Recent Role: [YOUR TITLE AT COMPANY] - Relevant Achievement #1: [DESCRIBE WITH NUMBERS IF POSSIBLE] - Relevant Achievement #2: [DESCRIBE WITH NUMBERS IF POSSIBLE] - Why I Want This Specific Role: [BE HONEST AND SPECIFIC] - Why This Company: [WHAT GENUINELY INTERESTS YOU ABOUT THEM - PRODUCT, MISSION, CULTURE] - Something Personal That Connects: [OPTIONAL - A STORY, EXPERIENCE, OR VALUE THAT TIES TO THE COMPANY] Rules: 1. DO NOT start with "I am writing to apply for..." or "I am excited to apply..." Start with something that makes the reader want to keep reading -- a relevant story, a bold statement, or a specific insight about the company. 2. Keep it to 3-4 paragraphs, under 350 words. 3. Paragraph 1: Hook + why this company/role specifically (show you've done research). 4. Paragraph 2: Your most relevant achievement, told as a brief story with a measurable result. 5. Paragraph 3: How your skills map to their biggest need (reference the job posting directly). 6. Paragraph 4: Confident close with a call to action (not "I hope to hear from you"). 7. Match the company's tone (if they're a startup, be energetic; if they're a law firm, be polished). 8. Sound like a human being, not a form letter.

Tip: The cover letter's only job is to make them want to read your resume. It's not a resume summary -- it's your chance to show personality, motivation, and cultural fit that a resume can't convey.

14 Cold Email to Hiring Manager

My Details: - My Name: [YOUR NAME] - My Current Role: [TITLE] - Target Company: [COMPANY NAME] - Hiring Manager's Name: [NAME] - Hiring Manager's Title: [TITLE] - Department I'm Targeting: [DEPARTMENT] - Why This Company: [SPECIFIC REASON - not generic "I admire your mission"] - My Top Relevant Skill/Achievement: [ONE STRONG PROOF POINT] - Something I Noticed About the Company: [RECENT NEWS, PRODUCT LAUNCH, CHALLENGE THEY MIGHT HAVE] Requirements: 1.
