Your Relationship with Gravity

By Selena La Brooy • January 10, 2025

Gravity is the one force that never takes a day off. From the moment we stand up in the morning until we lie down at night, our bodies are in constant negotiation with the gravitational field. Most of us never think about this relationship, yet it was the central insight behind Dr. Ida Rolf’s life work and the foundation of everything we do in Rolfing Structural Integration.

Dr. Rolf understood something that seems simple but has profound implications. When the major segments of the body, the head, thorax, pelvis, and legs, are well aligned and balanced around a vertical axis, gravity flows through the body like a supportive force. The bones do the work of holding us up, and the muscles are free to do what they do best, which is to create movement. But when these segments are misaligned, shifted forward or back, tilted or rotated, the muscles have to take over the job of holding the body upright. That is exhausting work, and it is the source of much of the chronic pain and fatigue people experience.

Think of it this way. Imagine stacking a set of building blocks perfectly on top of one another. The stack is stable and requires no effort to maintain. Now shift one of the middle blocks slightly to the side. The blocks above it have to compensate, and the whole structure becomes less stable. It takes external support to keep it from toppling. In the body, that external support comes from muscles and fascia that are working overtime, day after day, just to keep you upright.

What does a well-organized body actually feel like? People who have experienced it often describe a sense of length and lift. There is a feeling of being taller without trying, as though the spine is gently lengthening upward. The shoulders rest easily without being held back. The head floats on top of the spine rather than being propped up by tense neck muscles. There is a quality of buoyancy, a sense that gravity is actually supporting you rather than dragging you down.

This is not an abstract ideal. It is something that can be felt and recognized in the body. Many of my clients describe it after their first few Rolfing sessions. They notice that standing feels easier, that walking has a quality of flow that was not there before. Some say they feel lighter, even though nothing about their weight has changed. What has changed is the relationship between their structure and the gravitational field.

The process of Rolfing works toward this by systematically releasing fascial restrictions that are holding the body in patterns of misalignment. Session by session, we work through the layers of the body, addressing the superficial fascia first and then moving deeper into the core structures. The goal is not to force the body into some ideal posture, but to remove the restrictions that are preventing it from finding its own natural balance.

Dr. Rolf famously said, “When the body gets working appropriately, the force of gravity can flow through. Then, spontaneously, the body heals itself.” This was not a mystical statement. It was a practical observation. When the body is well organized in gravity, circulation improves, breathing deepens, the nervous system calms, and the tissues receive better nourishment. The body’s own healing mechanisms function more efficiently when they are not burdened by the constant effort of fighting gravity.

One of the things I love about this work is watching the moment when a client first experiences that sense of effortless uprightness. There is often a look of surprise, as though they had forgotten what it felt like to simply stand without effort. For many people, the experience of being well organized in gravity is something they have not felt since childhood.

Gravity is not the enemy. It is a force that can either work for you or against you, depending on how your body is organized. Rolfing is, at its heart, about changing that relationship so that gravity becomes your ally. When it does, everything changes. Movement becomes easier, pain diminishes, and there is a quality of ease and vitality that permeates daily life.

If you have been carrying the weight of gravity as a burden rather than a support, I invite you to explore what is possible when that relationship shifts.
