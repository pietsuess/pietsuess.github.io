AI Prompts for Real Estate Agents: 8 ChatGPT Prompts That Close Deals

March 6, 2026 · 9 min read

Real estate agents spend an extraordinary amount of time writing. Listing descriptions, buyer follow-up emails, market update reports, social media posts, neighbourhood guides, cold outreach letters. The writing never stops, and most of it follows patterns you have repeated hundreds of times before.

ChatGPT can produce all of this in minutes. But here is the problem most agents run into: they type something like "write a listing description for a 3-bedroom house" and get back something so generic it could describe any house in any city. That kind of output is not just unhelpful, it is actively harmful. Buyers can spot AI-generated filler instantly, and it makes you look like you did not care enough to write something real.

The difference between useless AI output and genuinely useful output comes down to the prompt. A good prompt tells ChatGPT exactly what role to play, what tone to use, what format to follow, and what specific details to include. It is the difference between handing a stranger a pen and saying "write something" versus briefing a skilled copywriter with a detailed creative brief.

These 8 prompts are written specifically for real estate professionals. Each one is ready to copy, paste, and customize. Fill in the [BRACKETED] fields with your property and client details, and you will get output you would actually send.

Listing Descriptions

Prompt 1: Luxury Property Listing Description

Copy this prompt:
You are a luxury real estate copywriter who specializes in high-end residential properties. Write an MLS-ready listing description for the following property. The tone should be aspirational and sophisticated without being over-the-top. Avoid cliches like "stunning," "breathtaking," or "one-of-a-kind." Property details: - Address: [ADDRESS] - Price: [PRICE] - Bed/Bath: [BEDS] bed / [BATHS] bath - Square footage: [SQFT] - Lot size: [LOT SIZE] - Year built: [YEAR] - Key features: [LIST 5-8 STANDOUT FEATURES, e.g., chef's kitchen with Sub-Zero/Wolf appliances, primary suite with heated bathroom floors, 3-car garage with EV charging] - Neighbourhood highlights: [NEARBY AMENITIES, SCHOOLS, RESTAURANTS, GOLF COURSES, etc.] - Target buyer: [e.g., executive couple, downsizing empty-nesters, tech professional relocating from SF] Format requirements: - Opening hook (1 sentence that captures the lifestyle, not just the house) - 2-3 paragraphs covering the home's primary living spaces, outdoor areas, and neighbourhood context - Closing line with a soft call to action - Total length: 150-200 words (MLS character limits) - Do NOT start with the address
Why it works: This prompt bans overused luxury adjectives, forcing ChatGPT to find original language. Specifying the target buyer shapes the emotional appeal: a description for relocating tech executives emphasizes different features than one for downsizing empty-nesters. The MLS word count constraint keeps the output practical and ready to paste directly into your listing platform.

Prompt 2: Starter Home / First-Time Buyer Listing

Copy this prompt:
You are a real estate listing writer who understands first-time home buyers. Write a warm, encouraging listing description for a starter home. The tone should feel approachable and optimistic. Emphasize value, potential, and the lifestyle the neighbourhood supports for young professionals or growing families. Avoid language that sounds like the home needs work or is a compromise. Property details: - Address: [ADDRESS] - Price: [PRICE] - Bed/Bath: [BEDS] bed / [BATHS] bath - Square footage: [SQFT] - Key features: [LIST 4-6 FEATURES, e.g., updated kitchen, fenced backyard, new HVAC, walkable to transit] - Recent updates/improvements: [ANY RENOVATIONS OR NEW SYSTEMS] - Neighbourhood: [SCHOOLS, PARKS, TRANSIT, GROCERY, FAMILY-FRIENDLY DETAILS] - Target buyer: [e.g., first-time buyer couple in their late 20s, young family with one child] Format: - Headline (catchy, under 10 words) - 2 paragraphs, 120-170 words total - End with an invitation to book a showing - Mention monthly mortgage estimate only if: [YES/NO, and estimated payment if YES]
Why it works: First-time buyers are emotionally driven. This prompt tells ChatGPT to lead with optimism rather than square footage. The instruction to avoid "compromise" language prevents output that accidentally frames the home as lesser. The optional mortgage estimate adds a practical touch that resonates with buyers comparing renting versus owning.

Prompt 3: Investment Property Listing

Copy this prompt:
You are a commercial real estate copywriter experienced in investment property marketing. Write a listing description targeting real estate investors. Use data-driven, ROI-focused language. The tone should be confident and analytical, not flowery. Property details: - Address: [ADDRESS] - Asking price: [PRICE] - Property type: [e.g., duplex, fourplex, SFR rental, mixed-use] - Units: [NUMBER OF UNITS] - Current rental income (monthly gross): [AMOUNT] - Occupancy: [CURRENT OCCUPANCY RATE] - Cap rate: [CAP RATE or "calculate based on provided numbers"] - Recent capital expenditures: [NEW ROOF, UPDATED PLUMBING, etc.] - Market context: [RENT GROWTH TRENDS, NEIGHBOURHOOD DEVELOPMENT, ZONING CHANGES] - Value-add opportunity: [e.g., below-market rents, ADU potential, re-zoning possibility] Format: - Lead with the financial headline (cap rate, gross rent multiplier, or cash-on-cash return) - 2 paragraphs: financials/income first, then property condition and value-add potential - Close with "Contact for rent rolls and full financials" - 150-200 words - Do NOT use emotional or lifestyle language
Why it works: Investors scan listings for numbers, not narrative. This prompt forces ChatGPT to lead with cap rate and income data rather than describing the kitchen countertops. The explicit ban on emotional language prevents the model from defaulting to its standard real estate writing style, which is almost always buyer-facing and lifestyle-oriented.

Client Communication

Prompt 4: Buyer Follow-Up After Showing

Copy this prompt:
You are a buyer's agent writing a follow-up email after a property showing. The email should feel personal and attentive, not templated. Reference specific details from the showing to demonstrate you were paying attention to what the buyer cared about. Context: - Buyer name(s): [NAME(S)] - Property shown: [ADDRESS] - Asking price: [PRICE] - What the buyer seemed most interested in: [e.g., the backyard size, the school district, the finished basement, the natural light in the living room] - Concerns they raised: [e.g., the age of the roof, street noise, small master closet, distance from work] - Your honest assessment: [e.g., strong value for the area, likely multiple offers, would need new roof within 3 years] - Suggested next step: [e.g., submit offer, schedule second showing, view comparable property at 45 Oak St] Tone: Warm, professional, consultative. You are their trusted advisor, not a salesperson. Create gentle urgency without pressure. Format: - Subject line (reference the property, not generic) - 4-6 sentences in the body - End with a clear single next step and your availability - Sign off with: [YOUR NAME AND PHONE NUMBER]
Why it works: The magic is in the "what the buyer seemed most interested in" and "concerns they raised" fields. These two details transform a generic follow-up into a message that feels like it was written by someone who was genuinely listening. Buyers choose agents they trust, and trust is built by demonstrating attentiveness to the small things.

Prompt 5: Seller Market Update Email

Copy this prompt:
You are a listing agent sending a market update email to your seller client. The goal is to keep them informed, manage expectations, and reinforce confidence in your pricing strategy. Use actual data but explain it in plain language. Context: - Seller name: [NAME] - Property: [ADDRESS] - Current list price: [PRICE] - Days on market: [DOM] - Showings to date: [NUMBER] - Feedback themes from showings: [e.g., buyers love the kitchen but feel price is high, concerns about the busy street, most say it shows well] - Recent comparable sales: [LIST 2-3 COMPS WITH ADDRESS, SALE PRICE, DOM, AND KEY DIFFERENCES] - Your recommendation: [e.g., hold price for 2 more weeks, reduce by $15K, schedule open house, improve staging in master bedroom] - Current market conditions: [e.g., buyer's market, low inventory, rising rates slowing activity] Tone: Confident, transparent, data-backed. You are the expert, but you respect the seller's decision-making authority. Format: - Subject line referencing their property - Quick summary paragraph (market conditions + their property's position) - Comparable sales section (brief, 2-3 bullet points) - Your recommendation with reasoning (1 paragraph) - Close with invitation to call and discuss - Under 250 words total
Why it works: Seller communication is where agents win or lose listings. This prompt structures the email around data and strategy rather than vague reassurance. Including the comparable sales and your specific recommendation gives ChatGPT enough context to generate an email that sounds like it came from an agent who has done the analysis, not one sending a form letter.

Marketing

Prompt 6: Neighbourhood Guide

Copy this prompt:
You are a local real estate expert and lifestyle writer. Write a neighbourhood guide that a buyer's agent could send to relocating clients. The guide should read like a knowledgeable friend describing the area, not a Wikipedia article. Neighbourhood: [NEIGHBOURHOOD NAME, CITY] Include these sections: 1. The Vibe (2-3 sentences capturing what it feels like to live there) 2. Food & Drink (3-5 specific restaurants/cafes/bars with one-line descriptions) 3. Outdoors & Recreation (parks, trails, fitness, weekend activities) 4. Schools ([LIST TOP SCHOOLS AND RATINGS, or say "not applicable"]) 5. Commute ([COMMUTE TIMES TO KEY EMPLOYMENT CENTRES]) 6. The Market Right Now (1-2 sentences: [MEDIAN PRICE, PRICE TREND, INVENTORY LEVEL]) Tone: Conversational, insider knowledge. Use specific names of places, not generic categories. If you don't know a specific place, use [PLACEHOLDER - insert local spot] so I can fill it in. Format: Use the section headers above. Keep total length under 400 words. No bullet points in the Vibe section; use flowing prose.
Why it works: Neighbourhood guides are one of the highest-value pieces of content an agent can share, and they are perfect for ChatGPT because you can supply the local details while the AI handles the structure and polish. The instruction to use placeholder brackets for unknown specifics prevents ChatGPT from hallucinating restaurant names, which is the biggest risk with location-based content.

Prompt 7: Open House Social Media Post

Copy this prompt:
You are a real estate social media manager. Write an Instagram/Facebook post promoting an upcoming open house. The post should stop the scroll, highlight the most compelling features, and drive attendance. Open house details: - Property: [ADDRESS] - Date/Time: [DATE AND TIME RANGE] - Price: [PRICE] - Key features (pick top 3): [e.g., renovated kitchen, private pool, 0.5-acre lot in top school district] - What makes this one special: [THE ONE THING THAT WOULD MAKE SOMEONE DRIVE ACROSS TOWN TO SEE IT] - Target audience: [e.g., move-up buyers, families, investors, first-time buyers] Format: - Opening hook (first line must be compelling; this is what shows before "...more") - 3-5 lines of body copy highlighting features - Date, time, address clearly formatted - 3-5 relevant hashtags (mix of local and real estate) - Optional: suggest a carousel photo order (which rooms/angles to feature in what order) Rules: - No ALL CAPS except for the address line - No excessive exclamation marks (max 1 in entire post) - Do not use the word "gorgeous" - Keep under 150 words
Why it works: The "what makes this one special" field forces you to identify the single strongest selling point before you even run the prompt. That focus carries through the entire post. The style rules (no ALL CAPS, limited exclamation marks, banned words) prevent the over-the-top tone that makes real estate social media feel spammy and causes audiences to tune out.

Prompt 8: Cold Outreach to FSBO Seller

Copy this prompt:
You are a real estate agent reaching out to a For Sale By Owner (FSBO) seller. Write a first-contact email that is respectful of their decision to sell independently while clearly communicating the value a listing agent provides. This is NOT a hard sell. The goal is to start a conversation. Context: - Seller's name: [NAME, or "Homeowner" if unknown] - Property address: [ADDRESS] - Their asking price: [PRICE] - How long it's been listed FSBO: [DAYS/WEEKS] - One specific observation about their listing: [e.g., photos could be improved, price is competitive for the area, great curb appeal, listing is missing sq footage info] - Your key differentiator: [e.g., sold 14 homes in this neighbourhood last year, average 3% above asking, specialize in this price range] - Your market: [CITY/NEIGHBOURHOOD] Tone: Respectful, peer-to-peer, zero pressure. Acknowledge their effort. Position yourself as a resource, not a rescuer. Format: - Subject line that does NOT say "FSBO" or "For Sale By Owner" (sellers flag these as spam) - 5-7 sentences maximum - One specific, helpful observation about their listing or the market (give value upfront) - One clear credential or proof point - Close with a low-commitment ask (e.g., "Would a free comparative market analysis be useful?" not "When can we meet?") - Sign with name, phone, and one-line credential
Why it works: Most FSBO outreach templates are transparently self-serving, and sellers delete them immediately. This prompt is built around respect and value-first positioning. The instruction to give a specific observation about their listing before asking for anything demonstrates genuine expertise. The low-commitment close (a free CMA, not a listing appointment) dramatically increases response rates because it removes the risk for the seller.

Why These Prompts Work Better Than Generic Ones

If you have tried ChatGPT before and been disappointed, it was almost certainly a prompt problem, not an AI problem. There is a reason the prompts above are long and specific. Each one uses four techniques that generic prompts miss entirely.

Role-setting tells ChatGPT who it is before it starts writing. "You are a luxury real estate copywriter" produces fundamentally different output than "write a listing description." The model draws on different vocabulary, different structures, and different assumptions about the audience.

Output format constraints (word count, section structure, what to include and exclude) prevent the model from rambling or defaulting to a generic five-paragraph essay. MLS descriptions need to be tight. Social posts need to be scannable. Investor listings need numbers up front. Format is not a nice-to-have; it is what makes the output usable without heavy editing.

Tone control is the difference between sounding like a real professional and sounding like AI. Banning specific words ("stunning," "gorgeous," "one-of-a-kind") forces originality. Specifying "consultative, not salesperson" or "peer-to-peer, zero pressure" shapes the emotional register of every sentence.

Context specificity is what makes the output feel like yours. When you fill in the bracketed fields with real details about real properties, real buyer reactions, and real market data, ChatGPT has enough raw material to generate something that sounds like it came from an agent who knows the property and the market. That is the entire game.
